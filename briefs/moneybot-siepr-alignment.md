# MoneyBot x Financial Literacy Research Alignment Strategy

**Date:** May 28, 2026
**Topic:** Aligning MoneyBot's product strategy with GFLEC, FINRA, and Stanford SIEPR research.

## 1. The Narrative: How MoneyBot Relates to the Research
The prevailing data reveals a stark dichotomy:
*   **The Policy Surge:** 25 states now mandate K-12 personal finance courses (per NGPF data).
*   **The Capability Crisis:** Adult financial literacy is stagnant at 49%, and Gen Z is alarmingly low at 38% (TIAA Institute-GFLEC Personal Finance Index).

**MoneyBot's Positioning:** MoneyBot is the private-sector policy response for the "lost demographic." Millions of Millennials and Gen Z adults have already graduated and are navigating a complex financial system without the K-12 mandates protecting the next generation. MoneyBot is the post-grad intervention.

## 2. The Data: How MoneyBot Proves the Research
To prove the research, MoneyBot must act as a real-time telemetry engine for financial capability, effectively running applied versions of Stanford SIEPR studies:

*   **Baseline the "Big Five":** Integrate variations of the GFLEC "Big Five" financial literacy questions into onboarding via gamified, conversational prompts rather than a sterile survey.
*   **Quantify the "Fragility Tax":** Annamaria Lusardi's research highlights the cost of illiteracy (high borrowing costs, overdrafts, scams). MoneyBot can calculate a personalized "Fragility Tax" for users by analyzing their debt structure and showing them the literal dollar amount illiteracy is costing them.
*   **A/B Testing Interventions:** Track user decision-making *before* and *after* interacting with MoneyBot's modules. If a user's simulated credit score or savings rate improves after a 2-minute module, MoneyBot generates proprietary data proving that micro-learning works.

## 3. The Product: How MoneyBot Solves the Problem
MoneyBot operationalizes Stanford SIEPR's most critical finding: that **2-minute storytelling and gamification are the most effective interventions.**

*   **Just-in-Time Education:** Traditional K-12 mandates rely on "just-in-case" education (learning about mortgages at age 16). MoneyBot delivers "just-in-time" education (a 2-minute gamified narrative about auto loans while the user is physically at the dealership).
*   **Aesthetic as an Educational Tool:** Traditional financial education feels like homework. MoneyBot’s hyper-modern, Space Grotesk, pure-black (#000000) aesthetic signals that this is a premium, tech-forward tool, capturing Gen Z attention spans that traditional institutions cannot hold.
*   **Narrative over Numbers:** Replacing dense financial jargon with relatable, 2-minute scenario-based storytelling (e.g., "Choose your own adventure: The 24% APR Credit Card Trap").
