TIAA-GFLEC P-Fin Index 2026: Financial Literacy Hits Decade Low

Bottom Line

The 2026 TIAA Institute-GFLEC Personal Finance Index (P-Fin Index) marks its 10th anniversary with a sobering milestone: financial literacy among U.S. adults has declined to its lowest level in the survey's history. The drop is driven by a growing proportion of individuals with very limited financial knowledge, with Gen Z entering adulthood without foundational skills and women consistently scoring below men across nearly all personal finance areas.

Key Findings

  • Decade Low: Financial literacy has reached its lowest point in 10 years of tracking
  • Gen Z Crisis: More than one-third of Gen Z falls into the very lowest knowledge category
  • Gender Gap Persists: Women consistently score below men across nearly all functional areas of personal finance
  • Retirement Fluency Crisis: Adults average only 2 correct answers out of 6 retirement-related questions across all generations
  • Financial Fragility Correlation: Those scoring low are 4x more likely to struggle making ends meet and 3x more likely to be financially fragile

The P-Fin Index Framework

The survey measures knowledge across 8 functional areas using 28 questions: 1. Earning 2. Consuming 3. Saving 4. Investing 5. Borrowing/Managing Debt 6. Insuring 7. Comprehending Risk 8. Go-To Information Sources

What Works: The Roadmap Forward

The data points toward proven solutions:

  • Financial Education Works: Adults who received financial education score significantly higher than those who didn't
  • Higher Literacy = Better Outcomes: Strongly associated with regular saving, effective debt management, and retirement confidence
  • Retirement Fluency Impact: Among workers with higher retirement fluency:
  • 80%+ save for retirement regularly
  • 70% feel confident about comfortable retirement

Recommended Intervention Channels

  1. School-Based (K-12): Most powerful tool for reaching young people early
  2. Employer Wellness Programs: Scalable channel for working adults
  3. Community Initiatives: Close critical gaps among retirees
  4. Targeted Demographics: Address consistent low-scoring groups
  5. AI Tools: Emerging promise as accessible resources, especially for younger adults

New Tool: P-Fin 8

TIAA Institute and GFLEC developed an 8-question short quiz (gflec.org/pfin8) that produces a standardized measure of financial literacy — a practical tool for researchers, educators, and employers to evaluate programs and track progress over time.

Analysis

This 10-year dataset validates the urgency of the MoneyBot mission. The decline isn't just a static problem — it's actively worsening as financial products grow more complex (BNPL, gamified trading, algorithmic credit) while education lags. The P-Fin Index provides the empirical backbone for mandate advocacy: it quantifies the capability gap, identifies intervention points, and now offers a lightweight 8-question assessment tool (P-Fin 8) that schools and employers can deploy at scale.

The retirement fluency data is particularly alarming — averaging 2/6 correct across ALL generations, not just Gen Z. This suggests the problem isn't just about young people; it's a systemic failure across age groups.
