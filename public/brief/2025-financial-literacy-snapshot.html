2025 Financial Literacy & Education Snapshot

Date Compiled: May 27, 2026 Primary Sources: TIAA Institute-GFLEC, FINRA Investor Education Foundation, Next Gen Personal Finance (NGPF)

1. Overall Literacy Remains Stagnant at 49%

According to the 2025 TIAA Institute-GFLEC Personal Finance Index, U.S. adults correctly answered an average of only 49% of basic personal finance questions. * The Context: This number has remained effectively flat since the survey started in 2017, representing eight years of zero national progress. Individuals with low financial literacy are 3 times more financially fragile than those with high literacy. Comprehending risk remains the most poorly understood financial concept.

2. Gen Z is Entering the Economy with Critical Knowledge Gaps

The 2025 TIAA-GFLEC index isolated generational data, revealing that Gen Z correctly answered only 38% of the questions on average. * The Context: This is significantly lower than older age groups. They are entering an increasingly complex financial landscape with the weakest set of basic financial tools.

3. Basic Financial Capability is Actively Declining

The FINRA Foundation’s 6th wave of the National Financial Capability Study (published mid-2025) revealed a concerning reversal in household financial resilience. * The Context: After a 12-year period of sustained improvements in household financial capability, there is a distinct decline in U.S. adults' ability to make ends meet and save for emergencies. While wages held relatively steady, increased cost-of-living heavily strained middle-income households. (Note: 20% of U.S. adults are now actively interested in getting their financial advice from AI).

4. High School Education Mandates Are Surging in Response

According to Next Gen Personal Finance's (NGPF) 2025 State of Financial Education Report, exactly half the country (25 states) now mandates a guaranteed, standalone Personal Finance course for all public high school students prior to graduation. * The Context: This is a massive, rapid policy shift; in 2020, only 8 states had this requirement. NGPF tracks this as the single most effective intervention point for breaking generational cycles of financial illiteracy.
